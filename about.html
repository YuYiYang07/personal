2007 — 2014

seven

Born in Zhejiang, China. Before I wrote code, I practiced calligraphy — the discipline of brush and ink, the patience of getting a single stroke right.

My early life was defined by the flow of the Erhu and the rhythm of tradition. Computers were for “Word” and “Excel” only. I had zero exposure to programming.

But the precision of calligraphy and the patience of music were quietly building something — a way of thinking that would matter later.

2014 — 2022

fifteen

The world got bigger. Two wheels carried me further than my feet ever could — past the familiar streets, past the routines, into questions I didn’t yet know how to ask.

Adolescence was a slow accumulation: discipline from the arts, logic from mathematics, curiosity from everything in between.

I didn’t know where the road was leading. I just knew it stretched further than I could see.

2023 — 2024

seventeen

Moving to Singapore was a blank slate. New country, new language rhythms, new rules. I tackled the O-Levels with intensity.

“Python” was still just a snake to me. But the logical thinking I’d developed in math was quietly laying the groundwork for what was coming.

Summer roads, windows down, the feeling that everything was about to change — and it did.

2025 — present

nineteen

Entered Singapore Polytechnic, Diploma in AI & Analytics. In twelve months, I sprinted from “Hello World” to deploying YOLO AI models on Edge devices.

Semester GPA: 4.00 / 4.00. Full-stack development, embedded AI, robotics. WRO competitor, school speaker.

The ground fell away — and I discovered I could fly.
